Onboarding

Once the app has been installed, open WebForce and tap the purple + button. You'll be prompted to grant two permissions.

🔐
Two permissions are required: "Appear on top" and "Enable Volume detection". Both are needed for full functionality and neither invades user privacy.

Tap Allow now on both permission prompts. Once granted, the app is ready to use.

02

How it works

WebForce runs entirely in the background — it never visibly appears during a performance. You pre-load a forced URL into the app. When running, it listens for a triggering sequence and executes a command in response.

Two elements in play

Every effect relies on a trigger (how the app is activated) and a command (what happens once activated). Configure both before performing.

03

Triggers

The trigger is activated either just before handing the phone to the spectator, or right after they make their selection. Choose the method that fits your performance style.

📳
Shake
Shake the phone to trigger. Conceal with a natural arm gesture or by pointing at something.
🔄
Flip facedown
Place the phone facedown on a flat, stationary surface for 4–5 seconds. Works hands-free in the spectator's hands.
🔊
Volume up
Press the volume up button. Clean and discreet. The phone's actual volume is unaffected.

Shake sensitivity

The shake sensitivity threshold is customisable. Slide the sensitivity bar in the setup page to control how hard a shake is needed. You can test it live — the phone vibrates when the shake strength crosses the threshold.

✓
An optional vibration indicator is available for Shake and Volume up — it vibrates the phone in your hand to confirm the trigger fired successfully during the effect.
⚠
Flip facedown only works on a flat, stationary surface. Simply turning the phone upside down will not trigger it — this is intentional to prevent accidental activations.
04

Commands

Once the trigger fires, the app executes one of two commands. Choose based on whether the spectator is scrolling or tapping to make their selection.

Open URL immediately
Instantly opens the pre-loaded URL once triggered. Best for effects where the spectator only scrolls — no tapping involved.
Enable overlay
Casts an invisible full-screen button. When the spectator taps anywhere, the forced URL opens. Scrolling temporarily dismisses the overlay.

Understanding the overlay

The overlay is a transparent button covering the entire screen. Once the trigger fires, it activates. When the spectator scrolls, it dismisses for the configured duration (0.3s–5.0s), then reappears automatically.

Screen facedown
Use a shorter dismissal duration so the overlay reactivates quickly between scrolls.
0.3s – 1.5s
Screen face up
Use a longer duration so scrolls register naturally on the phone. Ensure enough pause before the spectator taps.
2.0s – 5.0s
Practice mode
Select the grey overlay colour in setup to make the overlay visible — lets you observe exactly when it activates and dismisses.
Grey colour
⚠
Always ensure enough time passes between the spectator's last scroll and their tap. If they tap immediately after scrolling, the overlay may still be dismissed and the force won't fire.
05

Setting up an effect

There are two ways to deploy WebForce — saving a preset for repeated use, or activating on the spot for impromptu performances.

Saving a preset

1
Tap the + button
From the presets screen, tap the purple + button to open the setup page.
2
Enter the force URL and configure settings
Paste your force URL, select a trigger method, and choose your command (Open URL or Overlay). Adjust sensitivity and timeout as needed.
3
Tap "Save as preset" and name it
Give the preset a meaningful name — for a Wikipedia word force, include the forced word and article name so you remember what to search for during performance.
4
To perform, tap the preset
Selecting a saved preset arms the effect and hides the app automatically. The effect has started.
💡
You can sort presets by recently edited, alphabetically, or in a custom order. Search is also available to quickly find a specific preset during performance.

Impromptu setup

Follow the same steps as saving a preset, but instead of "Save as preset", tap Activate without saving. The app hides itself and the effect begins immediately. Note that the configuration will not be saved after performing.

Effect tutorials

Google image force Easy

Force any image from a Google Images search. The spectator scrolls through real results and taps freely — but always lands on your chosen image.

1
Copy the shareable URL of your forced image
Paste it into WebForce. Select Shake or Volume up as trigger and Enable overlay as command.
2
Introduce the phone and search the category
Show the spectator genuine images (e.g. search "landmarks" if forcing the Eiffel Tower). Let them see the variety.
3
Activate the trigger just before handing over
Shake or press volume up. The overlay is now active.
4
Instruct the spectator to scroll facedown and tap
Ensure the dismissal duration has passed after their last scroll before they tap. The forced image opens.
Tutorial — Google image force

Wikipedia word force Advanced

Force a specific word from a Wikipedia article. The spectator scrolls to a "random" spot, places the phone down, and the article switches invisibly to show your forced word at the top.

1
Find a Wikipedia article with large blocks of text
Choose an article with minimal images. A good starting point: History of Poland (1945–1989)
2
Highlight a comma or full stop near your target word
Pick something small and discreet. The highlight anchors the share URL to that exact position in the article.
3
Tap Share and copy the URL
Open the URL in a browser and check which word appears at the top of the page — that is your forced word.
4
Configure: Flip facedown + Open URL immediately
Paste the URL into WebForce. The flip facedown trigger fires passively while the spectator places the phone down — no action required from you.
5
Perform: let them scroll to a random spot, then place the phone down
Guide them: "Scroll somewhere random with a bunch of words — avoid pictures. Once you feel like you've landed somewhere random, just put the phone down." The switch happens in the 10 seconds they place it on the table. Use this time to recap the selection process.
✓
If the forced word is too short or weak, try highlighting a different comma in the article. It's a trial-and-error process — keep going until you get a strong word.
